Biological Experience

  • Brazos Water Snake Life History Study
    • A comprehensive life history study of the Brazos Water Snake for the Brazos River Authority. Field work included passively trapping for snakes, actively searching for snakes, capturing snakes by hand and with tools, recording morphological measurements of captured snakes, performing snake fungal disease swabbing, assisting in PIT tagging and blood drawing of captured snakes, boat operation, and river navigation. Office duties included data entry and analysis. Contracted with Brazos River Authority.
  • Edwards Aquifer Authority Habitat Conservation Plan
    • A Multiyear Habitat Conservation Plan taking place in the San Marcos and Comal Rivers as well as their respective headwaters. Field work included Drop netting and dip netting to record quantities as well as measurements of the endangered Fountain Darter. Water Quality assessments as well as flow measurements were also conducted
  • East Texas Mussel Project
    • Freshwater Mussel inventories in east Texas and Louisiana aquatic systems. Field work involved actively searching for mussel species in freshwater systems by the means of diving, wading and snorkeling, as well as inventory and measurements of mussels captured. Contracted with Texas Comptroller.
  • Federal Mussel Permitting
    • Freshwater Mussel inventories in central Texas aquatic systems. Field work involved actively searching for mussel species in freshwater systems by the means of diving, wading and snorkeling, as well as inventory and measurements of mussels captured. Contracted with Brazos River Authority.
  • GBRA Ecological Assessment
    • Larval fish population assessments and coastal bird surveys in the Guadalupe River Delta for the Guadalupe Blanco River Authority. Field work included using throw traps to capture larval fish within transects, as well as point counts for coastal birds. Office duties included identification of fish and crustacean species using dichotomous keys. Contracted with Guadalupe Blanco River Authority.
  • Permian Highway Pipeline
    • Biological monitoring in active construction zone for Kinder Morgan. Duties included clearing right-of-way for construction, identification and relocation of herpetofauna found on right-of-way and ensuring client is staying within biological compliance to mitigate impact on native Houston Toad populations. Contracted with Kinder Morgan.
